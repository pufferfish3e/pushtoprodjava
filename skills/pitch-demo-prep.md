# Pitch and Demo Prep Skill

## Use this when

- preparing the final walkthrough
- deciding what to show live
- aligning the verbal framing with the product scope

## Goal

Make the product demo feel sharp, deliberate, and grounded in a real internal workflow problem.

## Core demo sequence

1. State the SPSU coordination problem clearly.
2. Show the cross-event dashboard.
3. Paste messy notes.
4. Run Claude extraction.
5. Show tasks appear with urgency and blockers.
6. Ask one Genspark question.
7. Close on visibility and time saved.

## What to rehearse

- who speaks first
- who pastes the notes
- who clicks the process button
- who asks the Genspark question
- which task and blocker to point at

## Rules for the live demo

- do not improvise the sample notes
- do not improvise the natural-language query
- do not add features in the final 30 minutes
- if a stretch feature is shaky, do not foreground it

## Good closing points

- real SPSU workflow pain
- zero extra data entry for secretaries beyond paste and review
- live visibility for CC / VCC users
- Claude handles extraction and prioritization
- Genspark makes status readable in plain English

## Acceptance checklist

- the full demo can be run end to end
- the story matches the actual shipped scope
- every teammate knows what the strongest judging moment is
